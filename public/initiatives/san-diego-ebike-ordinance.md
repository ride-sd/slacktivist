# Information

San Diego Councilmember Campillo has introduced an ordinance that would ban children under 12 from riding Class 1 and Class 2 e-bikes — the slower, pedal-assist category that tops out at 20 mph. The proposal is headed to the Public Safety and Livable Neighborhoods Committee.

**Why this ordinance is problematic:**

- The staff report's entire factual basis is a single local TV news segment — no SDPD collision data, no hospital reports, no breakdown by bike class or rider age
- California already restricts Class 3 e-bikes (28 mph) to riders 16+, but chose not to impose age limits on Class 1/2 bikes because the evidence didn't support it
- Dangerous behaviors cited — running red lights, riding without a helmet — are already illegal and could be addressed through enforcement
- The City's own Climate Action Plan encourages e-bike use; this ordinance pushes kids into car trips instead
- Cycling advocates and e-bike-using families were not included in stakeholder outreach

# Content

I oppose the proposed e-bike ordinance from Councilmember Campillo. The staff report's entire evidentiary basis is a single local TV news article — no SDPD collision data, no published hospital report, no breakdown by rider age or e-bike class.

That breakdown matters. The ordinance targets Class 1 and Class 2 e-bikes, which top out at 20 mph. California already imposes a minimum age of 16 on Class 3 bikes at 28 mph — and found no such restriction warranted for slower classes. Before going further than the state, the District should answer: how many injuries involved Class 1 or Class 2 bikes specifically, and how many of those riders were under 12?

The dangerous behaviors cited — running red lights, riding without helmets, ignoring stop signs — are already illegal. Enforce existing law rather than adding new restrictions on kids who are riding responsibly.

Finally, the staff report lists "parents and community members" as stakeholders but discloses nothing about what they said. Which groups were consulted? Did they support this? The Committee should require that accounting before this moves forward.
