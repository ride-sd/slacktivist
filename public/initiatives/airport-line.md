# Information

SANDAG has long deliberated and promised a rail connection the airport. Of the alternatives presented, none are more useful than short airport-downtown shuttles, with the Trolley alternative actually kneecapping the system going forward. The Airport Line has lots of potential, and we need to ensure that it lives up to it. By pushing for a people mover, we ensure less interlining (which reduces capacity and reliability) while also allowing for far more expansion potential.

Why an APM is the obvious choice:

- **Frequency** - Can run up to every 2 minutes, a transformational frequency
- **Reliability** - Will have its own set of tracks, ensuring greater reliability
- **Trolley benefits** - If the Airport Line runs on its own tracks, the Trolley's busiest lines can run even more frequency. Long term the Blue and Green Lines will deserve 5 minute frequency. This is only possible if the Airport Line runs on its own tracks.
- **Potential extensions** - Built as an APM, the Airport Line can expand down Market St, a corridor with relatively easy constructability and ridership potential.

What they're offering instead: Short people mover shuttles to downtown and an interlined Trolley. Both of these options are short-sighted. Downtown is a big destination, but not the only one. A people mover that extends beyond downtown can serve the most people efficiently. As luggage is inconvenient to carry around, there is a steep penalty to transferring, which is why a one seat ride is important here. An interlined Trolley concept will wreck long term frequency visions that we will need if we are serious about transit in San Diego.

# Content

Dear SANDAG Board Members,

I am writing to urge you to ensure that the Airport Line doesn't cripple the future needs of the Trolley, while also taking into account rider needs.

While an interlined Trolley proposal is appealing at a surface level, it will kneecap the frequencies the Blue and Green Line will need in the future while simultaneously introducing reliability issues that can cascade across the system. This is exactly the problem the Copper Line was introduced to prevent, so why would we reintroduce it on a flagship project?

The obvious choice is an automated people mover (APM).
APMs run on their own dedicated grade-separated trackage and have a suite of advantages over a regular Trolley. Per the SANDAG ATC Concept study, an APM can run at a frequency of every 2 minutes, which would be a giant improvement over the Trolley's 15 minute frequencies.

Speed: Grade-separated tracks mean no stopping at traffic lights
Capacity: Ability to move thousands of people per hour
Reliability: Not impeded by car traffic, congestion, or other trolleys

If we're investing huge sums into transit infrastructure, we need to build something that isn't expected to just be a short shuttle. While downtown is a very big destination, it's not the only one. With luggage being challenging to carry around, one seat rides become far more important on airport lines, which is why it is a shame that none of the proposed concepts leave downtown.

There is a convenient corridor for an APM to extend down: Market St.
Market St is a dense and underserved corridor with a large and convenient median to place an elevated APM upon. It can run all the way to Euclid Ave, where it would have an interchange with the Orange and Purple Lines. This would vastly open up the reach of the Airport Line, enabling many trips that would otherwise be inconvenient, all while boosting transit accessability to dense, low-income neighborhoods that depond upon it.

My Ask:

Please commit to an APM to avoid the pitfalls of further interlining the Trolley. San Diego deserves a transit system that plans for the future, not one that cripples itself to save a buck. APMs have advantages in frequency, grade-separation, and scalability. Additionally, explore a routing that takes the APM past downtown! Market St is an obvious corridor, with Euclid Ave serving as a very natural terminus. It makes no sense that it has never even be studied. This would make a lot of one and two seat rides possible, strongly increasing the Airport Line's utility. 

I am committed to staying engaged on this issue and hope SANDAG will make the right choices about the mode and routing of the Airport Line.

Thank you for your consideration.
