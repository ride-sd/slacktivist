# Information

San Diego deserves better! The LOSSAN railway is a vital link between San Diego and Los Angeles (and beyond). Currently, trains traveling along the railway are exposed to catastrophic failure on the unstable and collapsing bluffs in Del Mar. While hundreds of millions have been poured into stop-gap solutions, a tunnel is being planned to ensure long term stability. However, local interest groups and freight railroads have opposed the tunnel and are threatening to make it too small to ensure future electrification. 

Medium and long term, electrification will be a humungous improvement to service, and we need to ensure the tunnel gets built with a: a good alignment, and b: space for electrification. Same story for the planned Miramar Hill tunnel which adds a station at UTC while skipping a very circuitous alignment.

Additionally, planned new stations at Camp Pendleton, Del Mar Fairgrounds, UTC, and Balboa Avenue will transform the utility of the Coaster.

- **Resiliency**: The Del Mar Tunnel will ensure the critical railway doesn't fall into the ocean.
- **Speed**: Del Mar and Miramar Hill tunnels will shave 15 minutes off the existing route, speeding up trips for everyone.
- **New Stations**: New stations at Camp Pendleton, Del Mar Fairgrounds, UTC, and Balboa Avenue help ensure that the Coaster is useful for everybody and enhance connectivity.
- **Future Electrification**: We must ensure that the new tunnels will be compatible with electrification.
